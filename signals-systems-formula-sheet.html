CLO 2

Convolution

Zero-state response of an LTI system — direct & graphical

Convolution Integral Ch 2

The zero-state response of a continuous-time LTI system is the input convolved with the impulse response \(h(t)\).

Definition (both equivalent forms)
\[ y(t)=x(t)*h(t)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\! x(\tau)\,h(t-\tau)\,d\tau=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\! x(t-\tau)\,h(\tau)\,d\tau \]
Causal input & system
\[ y(t)=\int_{0}^{t} x(\tau)\,h(t-\tau)\,d\tau \]

Both \(x\) and \(h\) are zero for \(t<0\).

Convolution with an impulse
\[ x(t)*\delta(t)=x(t),\qquad x(t)*\delta(t-T)=x(t-T) \]
Algebraic properties
\[ \begin{aligned} &\text{Commutative:}\ \ x*h=h*x\\ &\text{Distributive:}\ \ x*(h_1+h_2)=x*h_1+x*h_2\\ &\text{Associative:}\ \ x*(h_1*h_2)=(x*h_1)*h_2 \end{aligned} \]
Shift & width properties
\[ \begin{aligned} &x(t-T_1)*h(t-T_2)=c(t-T_1-T_2),\ \ c=x*h\\[4pt] &\text{width: } w_y=w_x+w_h \end{aligned} \]
Useful closed-form results
\[ \begin{aligned} e^{\lambda_1 t}u(t)*e^{\lambda_2 t}u(t)&=\frac{e^{\lambda_1 t}-e^{\lambda_2 t}}{\lambda_1-\lambda_2}\,u(t),\ \ \lambda_1\neq\lambda_2\\[6pt] u(t)*u(t)&=t\,u(t) \end{aligned} \]
Graphical convolution — procedure
  1. Reflect \(h(\tau)\) about the vertical axis → \(h(-\tau)\).
  2. Shift \(h(-\tau)\) by \(t\) → \(h(t-\tau)\).
  3. Multiply: \(x(\tau)\,h(t-\tau)\).
  4. Integrate (area) over \(\tau\) for each \(t\) to get \(y(t)\).
CLO 3

Fourier Analysis

Series for periodic signals, transform for aperiodic signals, & sampling

Trigonometric Fourier Series Ch 6

For a periodic signal of period \(T_0\); fundamental frequency \(\omega_0=\dfrac{2\pi}{T_0}\).

Series
\[ x(t)=a_0+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\big[a_n\cos(n\omega_0 t)+b_n\sin(n\omega_0 t)\big] \]
Coefficients (integrate over one period)
\[ a_0=\frac{1}{T_0}\!\int_{T_0}\! x(t)\,dt,\quad a_n=\frac{2}{T_0}\!\int_{T_0}\! x(t)\cos(n\omega_0 t)\,dt,\quad b_n=\frac{2}{T_0}\!\int_{T_0}\! x(t)\sin(n\omega_0 t)\,dt \]
Compact (amplitude–phase) form
\[ x(t)=C_0+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}C_n\cos(n\omega_0 t+\theta_n) \]
Conversions
\[ C_0=a_0,\quad C_n=\sqrt{a_n^{2}+b_n^{2}},\quad \theta_n=-\tan^{-1}\!\frac{b_n}{a_n} \] \[ a_n=C_n\cos\theta_n,\qquad b_n=-C_n\sin\theta_n \]

Exponential Fourier Series Ch 6

Synthesis & analysis
\[ x(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}D_n\,e^{jn\omega_0 t} \] \[ D_n=\frac{1}{T_0}\!\int_{T_0}\! x(t)\,e^{-jn\omega_0 t}\,dt \]
Relation to trigonometric coefficients
\[ \begin{aligned} D_0&=a_0=C_0\\ D_n&=\tfrac{1}{2}(a_n-jb_n)=\tfrac{C_n}{2}\,e^{\,j\theta_n}\\ D_{-n}&=\tfrac{1}{2}(a_n+jb_n)=\tfrac{C_n}{2}\,e^{-j\theta_n} \end{aligned} \]
Magnitude & phase link
\[ C_n=2\,|D_n|\ \ (n\ge 1),\qquad \theta_n=\angle D_n \]
Parseval's theorem (power)
\[ P_x=\frac{1}{T_0}\!\int_{T_0}\!|x(t)|^2 dt=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}|D_n|^2=C_0^{2}+\tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}C_n^{2} \]

The Fourier Transform Ch 7

For aperiodic (energy) signals. Convention used here: \(\operatorname{sinc}(x)=\dfrac{\sin x}{x}\) (Lathi). Check your notes if a different sinc is used.

Analysis (forward)
\[ X(\omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\! x(t)\,e^{-j\omega t}\,dt \]
Synthesis (inverse)
\[ x(t)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\! X(\omega)\,e^{\,j\omega t}\,d\omega \]

Sampling — Continuous to Discrete Ch 8

For a signal band-limited to \(B\) Hz (i.e. \(\omega_{\max}=2\pi B\)).

Sampling theorem (Nyquist)
\[ f_s\ge 2B,\qquad \omega_s=\frac{2\pi}{T},\qquad T\le\frac{1}{2B} \]

Nyquist rate \(=2B\); Nyquist interval \(=\tfrac{1}{2B}\); aliasing occurs if \(f_s<2B\).

Spectrum of the sampled signal
\[ \overline{X}(\omega)=\frac{1}{T}\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}X(\omega-n\omega_s) \]

The spectrum repeats every \(\omega_s\); reconstruction needs an ideal lowpass filter.

Discrete-Time & z-Transform

DT systems, the z-transform, difference equations, DTFS/DTFT, DFT & FFT

Discrete-Time Signals & Systems Ch 3

Convolution sum (zero-state response)
\[ y[n]=x[n]*h[n]=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}x[k]\,h[n-k]=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n-k]\,h[k] \]
Impulse properties
\[ x[n]*\delta[n]=x[n],\qquad x[n]*\delta[n-m]=x[n-m] \]
Geometric sums — your safety net
\[ \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} r^{n}=\frac{1-r^{N}}{1-r}\ (r\neq 1),\qquad \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^{n}=\frac{1}{1-r}\ (|r|<1) \]

Use these to derive any z-transform from the definition.

The z-Transform Ch 5

Bilateral
\[ X(z)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,z^{-n} \]
Unilateral (causal)
\[ X(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}x[n]\,z^{-n} \]

Difference Equations & Transfer Function Ch 5

General LCCDE
\[ \sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k\,y[n-k]=\sum_{k=0}^{M}b_k\,x[n-k] \]
Transfer function
\[ H(z)=\frac{Y(z)}{X(z)}=\frac{\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{M}b_k\,z^{-k}}{\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k\,z^{-k}} \]
Responses
\[ \begin{aligned} \text{Zero-state: }& Y(z)=H(z)\,X(z)\\ \text{Impulse: }& h[n]=\mathcal{Z}^{-1}\{H(z)\} \end{aligned} \]
Solving with initial conditions: take the unilateral z-transform of both sides, substitute the left-/right-shift properties (which inject \(y[-1],y[-2],\dots\)), solve algebraically for \(Y(z)\), then invert via partial fractions using the pairs above.

DTFS & DTFT Ch 9

Discrete-Time Fourier Series — period \(N\), \(\Omega_0=\tfrac{2\pi}{N}\)
\[ x[n]=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}D_k\,e^{\,jk\Omega_0 n} \] \[ D_k=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x[n]\,e^{-jk\Omega_0 n} \]

\(D_k\) is periodic: \(D_{k+N}=D_k\).

Discrete-Time Fourier Transform
\[ X(\Omega)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,e^{-j\Omega n} \] \[ x[n]=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{2\pi}\! X(\Omega)\,e^{\,j\Omega n}\,d\Omega \]

\(X(\Omega)\) is periodic with period \(2\pi\).

DFT & FFT Ch 8 · 9

\(N\)-point DFT (analysis)
\[ X[k]=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x[n]\,e^{-j\frac{2\pi}{N}kn},\quad k=0,\dots,N-1 \]
Inverse DFT (synthesis)
\[ x[n]=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}X[k]\,e^{\,j\frac{2\pi}{N}kn},\quad n=0,\dots,N-1 \]
Twiddle factor & matrix form
\[ W_N=e^{-j\frac{2\pi}{N}}\ \Rightarrow\ X[k]=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x[n]\,W_N^{\,kn} \]

DFT = samples of the DTFT: \(X[k]=X(\Omega)\big|_{\Omega=2\pi k/N}\).

FFT
\[ \text{operations: }\ O(N^{2})\ \longrightarrow\ O\!\left(N\log_2 N\right) \]

Fast algorithm for the DFT; radix-2 requires \(N=2^{m}\) (decimation in time / frequency).
